# RULES

## Work

- Start from nothing. Add only what survives scrutiny.
- Every rule earns its place.
- The smallest change wins.
- Architecture decisions go through the maintainer. Never vibe architecture.
- The bigger the interface, the weaker the abstraction.
- Make the zero value useful.
- A little copying is better than a little dependency.
- Clear is better than clever.
- Errors are values.
- Don't just check errors, handle them gracefully.
- Design the architecture, name the components, document the details.
- Documentation is for users.

## Philosophy of Software Design

- Strategy is better than tactics.
- The best way to get a good idea is to have lots of ideas.
- Interfaces must be simple and obvious.
- Strive for deep modularity, not shallow modularity.
- Decomposition is the key to addressing complexity.
- Design it twice. Compare the two. Build a third with what is learned from the two.
- Fitness functions are better than rules.
- Evolutionary architecture embraces change and is designed to evolve.

## Prose

- Say it once, then stop.
- Cut every word that doesn't earn its keep.
- No hedges. No apologies. No fillers. No fluff. No waffle. No weasel words.
- If you believe it, say it. If you don't, cut it.
- Lead with the verb.
- Delete the intro. Start in the middle.

## Shape

- Empty is better than padded.
- Specific is better than vague.
- Concrete is better than abstract.
- Names are better than descriptions.
